Welcome to Your Guide to Color Gaurd.

Color Guard is one of the sections in a marching band. The color gaurd's job is to provide a visual to tell the story of the music during a marching show. The color guard members do this by performing with flags, rifiles, and sabers.

There are three main peices of equipment the color guard uses

Flag

The flag is the main peice of equitment used in Gaurd. The flag consists of two parts; the pole and the silk. It is the first one that a member of color guard is taught to use and generally is the easiest to master.

Rifle

The rifle is harder to master than the flag. The rifle consists of three parts; the butt (which is the end), the neck (the part between the butt and the body), and the nose. Because it weighs 3 lbs and carries most of it's weight in the butt, a rifle is ideal for tossing.

Saber

The saber is almost the exact opposits of a rifle. The saber consists of the hilt and the blade. The saber is lightweight and most of the tosses on it are done from the hilt.
